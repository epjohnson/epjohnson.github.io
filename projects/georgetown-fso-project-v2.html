FSO Preparation Timeline

Month-by-month breakdown from now through FSO selection. This is the critical path.

Fall 2026 (Orientation Phase)

September
Settle into Georgetown, meet advisors, understand SFS structure. Begin light FSO background reading (Foreign Affairs articles, State Dept biographies).
October
Attend FSO recruitment panel. Ask State Dept recruiters about timeline, FSE dates, preparation. Sign up for FSO mailing list.
November
Meet with SFS Career Services. Ask: When does FSE registration open? Which prep courses do they recommend? How many candidates pass?
December
Light FSE prep. Read: "A Foreign Service Officer's Manifesto," FSE info booklet. Understand exam structure. Subscribe to foreign policy newsletters.

Spring 2027 (Internship Phase)

January
Finalize summer 2027 internship search. Reach out to 10-15 organizations (think tanks, Congressional offices). Have resumes reviewed.
February-March
Internship interviews and offers. Secure offer by end of March. Begin light FSE prep: read study guides, start vocabulary review.
April-May
Wrap spring semester. Conduct informational interviews with 2-3 FSO alumni (via Career Services). Ask about oral assessment specifics.

Summer 2027 (Internship Experience)

June-August
Execute summer internship. Focus on: (1) learning policy analysis, (2) building writing samples, (3) developing relationships with mentors, (4) getting strong recommendation letter.

Fall 2027 (FSE Prep Ramp-Up)

September
FSE Registration Opens (typically). Register immediately for June 2028 exam. Enroll in formal FSE prep course if available.
October
FSE prep course begins. Establish weekly study schedule (10-15 hours/week). Purchase FSE study materials (practice exams, study guides).
November-December
Intensive FSE prep. Read foreign policy journals, study current events (trade negotiations, sanctions, diplomacy). Take first practice exam. Identify weak areas.

Spring 2028 (Exam & Oral Prep)

January-March
Continue FSE prep. Take 2-3 more practice exams. Review weak areas. Light reading on U.S. government, diplomatic history. Schedule mock oral assessments with Career Services.
April-May
Final FSE prep push. Take final practice exam(s). Review all weak areas. Get adequate sleep. Begin oral presentation practice.
June
FOREIGN SERVICE EXAM (FSE) — Early June 2028. Submit, wait for results (~4-6 weeks). If you pass, oral assessment invitations begin.

Summer 2028 (Oral Assessment Phase)

July-August
Oral Assessment (if you pass FSE). Full-day assessment at State Dept facility. Group exercise, individual presentation, interview. Expect notification of pass/fail within 2-4 weeks.
August-September
If selected: Begin security clearance process (medical exam, background investigation). If not selected: Decide on next steps (reapply next year? alternate career path?).

Fall 2028 (Post-Selection)

September-December
Medical and security clearance. Complete senior year coursework. Expect assignment notification (where you'll be posted) by December 2028 / January 2029.

2029 (Onboarding)

May-June 2029
Graduate from Georgetown. Gap period: June-August 2029 (travel? rest? language refresher?)
August-September 2029
State Department Onboarding Begins. Report to State Dept facility for training and orientation. Then move to first posting (likely Asia, given your China focus).
Key Dates to Remember:
• September 2027: FSE Registration opens
• June 2028: FSE Exam
• July-August 2028: Oral Assessment (if you pass FSE)
• December 2028: Likely assignment notification
• August-September 2029: Start with State Dept

Summer Planning & Internship Strategy

Each summer is a chance to build your FSO candidacy. Here's how to maximize them.

Summer 2026 (Before Georgetown)
Focus
Transition to Georgetown. Refresh Mandarin. Explore Washington, DC. Familiarize yourself with policy community.
Suggested Activities
• 2-4 week Mandarin refresher/immersion
• Read: China's international role, current US-China relations
• Visit key Washington institutions: State Dept, Think tanks (CFR, Brookings)
• Network: Informational interviews with China-focused professionals
Outcome
Arrive at Georgetown prepared, energized, and with clear understanding of foreign service landscape.
Summer 2027 (Year 3 Internship)
Focus
Policy research. Writing. Build relationship with mentor who will write FSO recommendation letter.
Target Organizations
• Think tanks: CFR, Brookings, CSIS, Asia Society (China focus)
• Congressional offices: Economics committee staff, foreign relations
• International orgs: IMF, World Bank, Asian Development Bank
• State Dept (if available): Economics bureau or regional desk
Key Deliverables
• Write 2-3 policy memos or briefs (save for portfolio)
• Develop 1-2 substantive presentation(s)
• Build relationship with supervisor (coffee, lunch discussions)
• Secure strong recommendation letter
Summer 2028 (Year 4 Internship + Post-FSE)
Timeline Split
• June: FSE Exam
• July-mid August: Oral Assessment (if you pass FSE)
• Mid-August onward: Internship or break
If Selected for Orals
• Skip internship in June (prep for exam)
• Do oral assessment July-August
• Rest/travel/celebrate after orals
• Light internship or project in late August if energy allows
If Exam Goes Well
• Pursue ambitious internship: State Dept (Economics bureau), USAID, major think tank
• Focus on: international finance, sanctions, development economics, China policy
• Goal: Strengthen FSO profile before oral assessment
Internship Search Timeline:
• January 2027: Begin applications and networking
• February-March 2027: Interviews
• April 2027: Offers secured
• June-August 2027: Execute internship
